# Content Creation Guidelines

This document defines how every article should be written so that the journal remains:

- SEO optimized
- valuable and insightful
- calm and editorial in tone
- easy to read
- indistinguishable from human writing

Writers must follow these guidelines strictly.

---

# 1. Core Principles

Every article must aim to achieve three goals:

1. Provide genuine value to the reader
2. Be easy and pleasant to read
3. Rank well in search engines

Content should feel like a **thoughtful lifestyle magazine**, not like a marketing blog.

The writing must feel calm, reflective, and human.

---

# 2. Content Structure

Each article should follow a clear structure.

### Article Structure

1. Title
2. Short introduction
3. Context or framing section
4. Main sections with H2 headings
5. Examples or practical insights
6. Closing reflection

---

## Title

Titles must be:

- descriptive
- keyword focused
- clear about value

Examples:

Good titles

```
7 Cozy Bedroom Ideas for Small Apartments
How to Create a Calm Reading Corner at Home
Simple Evening Rituals That Bring Calm to Your Home
```

Avoid vague titles

```
Thoughts on Comfort
The Art of Living Well
Finding Peace
```

Readers must immediately understand what the article offers.

---

## Introduction

The introduction should:

1. acknowledge the reader's situation
2. introduce the topic
3. promise useful insights

Length guideline

60 to 120 words.

Example structure

Sentence 1
Describe the situation.

Sentence 2
Explain the context.

Sentence 3
Introduce what the article will show.

---

## Section Headings

Use clear H2 headings.

Example

```
Why Cozy Spaces Matter
Lighting That Creates Warmth
Using Texture to Add Comfort
Small Details That Change a Room
```

Headings should reflect search intent.

Avoid vague headings like:

```
More Ideas
Additional Thoughts
Extra Tips
```

---

## Paragraph Length

Paragraphs should be short.

Recommended

2 to 4 sentences.

Large blocks of text reduce readability.

---

## Lists

Use lists whenever possible.

Examples

- decorating tips
- setup steps
- routines
- checklists

Lists make content easier to scan.

---

## Closing Section

The article should end with a reflective or practical conclusion.

Examples

- a simple takeaway
- a small mindset shift
- a practical suggestion

Avoid promotional closing statements.

---

# 3. Tone and Style

The tone should feel calm, thoughtful, and editorial.

Content should resemble a **lifestyle magazine essay**.

Tone characteristics

- calm
- reflective
- warm
- observational
- grounded

Avoid hype.

Avoid phrases such as:

```
game changing
ultimate solution
revolutionary
life changing hack
```

Prefer natural observations.

Example

Instead of

"This trick will transform your home."

Write

"Small changes in lighting and texture can quietly change how a room feels."

---

# 4. Readability Level

Target reading level

Grade 6 to Grade 8.

Guidelines

- use simple sentence structures
- avoid technical vocabulary
- explain ideas with examples
- keep sentences under 20 words when possible

Readers should never struggle to understand the text.

---

# 5. Natural Human Writing

Content must read like it was written by a thoughtful human writer.

Writers should:

- vary sentence length
- mix short and medium sentences
- include observations and examples
- avoid mechanical transitions

Avoid predictable AI phrasing.

Examples to avoid

```
In today's fast paced world
When it comes to
Let's dive into
It is important to note
In conclusion
```

Let ideas flow naturally instead.

---

# 6. Prohibited Writing Patterns

The following rules must always be followed.

### DO NOT USE EM DASH

The em dash punctuation must never appear in content.

Use a comma or period instead.

Incorrect

```
Lighting matters — it changes the mood.
```

Correct

```
Lighting matters. It changes the mood.
```

---

### Avoid Common AI Sentence Patterns

Do not use the following structures.

```
In today's fast paced world
When it comes to
Let's dive into
In conclusion
This guide will explore
```

---

### Avoid Repetitive Structure

Do not repeat identical sentence patterns across paragraphs.

Example of bad pattern

```
One way to...
Another way to...
A third way to...
```

Use varied sentence structure.

---

### Avoid Marketing Tone

Do not use marketing language.

Examples to avoid

```
ultimate
best ever
game changer
revolutionary
```

---

# 7. SEO Guidelines

Each article should follow modern SEO practices.

---

## Primary Keyword

Each article must target one main keyword.

Examples

```
cozy bedroom ideas
balcony reading nook
candle decor ideas
```

The primary keyword must appear in:

- the title
- the first paragraph
- one heading
- the meta description

---

## Supporting Keywords

Include 3 to 5 related phrases.

Example

Primary keyword

```
cozy bedroom ideas
```

Supporting phrases

```
warm bedroom lighting
calm bedroom decor
neutral bedroom styling
```

Use them naturally.

Never stuff keywords.

---

## Heading Structure

Articles should contain:

```
1 H1
multiple H2
optional H3
```

Headings should contain meaningful phrases.

Example

```
Lighting Ideas for a Cozy Bedroom
```

---

## Internal Links

Every article should link to at least two other articles.

Example

An article about balcony spaces could link to:

```
reading nook ideas
evening rituals
```

Internal linking strengthens topical authority.

---

## Image Optimization

Images must include:

- descriptive filenames
- alt text

Example alt text

```
cozy reading nook with warm lighting
```

---

# 8. Value Guidelines

Each article must provide at least one of the following.

- practical tips
- design ideas
- psychological insights
- lifestyle rituals
- useful checklists

Readers should leave with something they can apply immediately.

Avoid generic content.

---

# 9. Readability Checklist

Before publishing confirm:

- paragraphs are short
- sentences are clear
- headings are descriptive
- ideas flow logically
- no em dash characters exist

---

# 10. SEO Checklist

Before publishing confirm:

- title includes the primary keyword
- first paragraph includes the primary keyword
- 3 to 5 related keywords appear naturally
- article length is at least 900 words when appropriate
- headings include meaningful phrases
- internal links exist

---

# Recommended Content Length for SEO

Search engines tend to favor content that covers a topic with enough depth. The length should support clarity and usefulness. It should never include filler.

Content must be long enough to explain the topic properly while remaining easy to read.

---

## Minimum Length

Short articles should only be written when the topic is very focused.

Recommended minimum length

700 words

This is appropriate for

- short essays
- focused reflections
- quick practical tips

Anything shorter usually struggles to rank unless the keyword competition is very low.

---

## Ideal Length for Most Articles

The best range for most SEO articles is

1200 to 1800 words

This length allows the article to

- explain the topic clearly
- include supporting keywords
- provide useful examples
- establish topical authority

Most practical guides and lifestyle articles should fall in this range.

Examples

- Cozy Bedroom Ideas
- Balcony Reading Nook Setup
- Evening Rituals for Calm Living

---

## Long Form Authority Content

Some articles should be designed as **pillar content**.

Recommended length

2000 to 3000 words

These articles should cover the topic comprehensively.

Examples

- Cozy Bedroom Setup Guide
- Small Balcony Cozy Living Guide
- How to Create a Calm Home Environment

These pillar articles should receive internal links from smaller articles.

---

## Word Count Rules

Do not increase length artificially.

Avoid

- repeating the same idea
- filler paragraphs
- unnecessary explanations

Content length must come from

- useful insights
- examples
- practical suggestions
- thoughtful observations

Quality always matters more than word count.

---

## Quick Reference

Short essay or reflection

700 to 900 words

Standard article

1200 to 1800 words

Pillar guide

2000 to 3000 words

---

# 11. Final Editorial Check

Before publishing ask these questions.

- Does the article read naturally?
- Does it sound like a thoughtful human wrote it?
- Does it avoid common AI sentence patterns?
- Does it offer genuine insight?
- Does it match the calm editorial tone of the journal?

If any section feels mechanical or generic, rewrite it.

The Cozy Living journal must always feel **human, thoughtful, and intentional**.
